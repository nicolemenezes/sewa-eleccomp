Electronic Components

Electronic components are the building blocks of electronic circuits. They control, modify, and amplify electrical signals in a circuit. Each component has a specific function, such as controlling the current, storing energy, or processing signals. When interconnected in a circuit board, they form useful electronic circuits.

Types of Electronic Components

An electrical circuit is an interconnection of electronic components. These elements are classified into active or passive based on their energy capabilities.

Active Components

Active components, such as transistors and operational amplifiers, manipulate electrical signals in a controlled manner. They are fundamental in circuits, enabling devices to perform various functions like switching, amplification, rectification, and feedback control.

Passive Components

Passive components, such as resistors, capacitors, and inductors, do not require external power and can only dissipate, store, or transfer energy. They play critical roles in energy storage, signal filtering, impedance matching, and current limiting.
